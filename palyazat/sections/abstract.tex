The project "Digital Preservation of Cultural Heritage using XR Technologies" aims to revolutionize how we interact with and preserve history. By leveraging Extended Reality (XR)—encompassing Virtual, Augmented, and Mixed Reality—this project focuses on creating high-fidelity digital twins of endangered cultural heritage sites. The proposal addresses the urgent need for non-destructive preservation and accessible education. Key outcomes include a standardized framework for digital heritage reconstruction, a cloud-based XR platform for remote exploration, and specialized tools for historical researchers. This summary highlights our interdisciplinary approach combining computer science with digital humanities to ensure that cultural artifacts remain accessible for future generations despite physical decay or environmental threats.
