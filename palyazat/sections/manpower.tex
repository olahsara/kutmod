\noindent
The consortium partners provide highly specialized manpower and expertise:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Arts et Métiers (CAPLAB / Laval Virtual):} Acts as the primary technology partner, offering engineering expertise in XR, VR/AR, and digital twins for industrial cooperation and technology transfer.
    \item \textbf{Springer Nature:} One of the world's largest scientific publishers, dedicating personnel to manage open access dissemination, data sharing, and maximizing the project's global impact.
    \item \textbf{Sapienza Università di Roma:} Provides researchers specialized in classical Greco-Roman archaeology, urban heritage conservation, and interdisciplinary monument protection theory.
    \item \textbf{National and Kapodistrian University of Athens (NKUA):} Contributes experts in classical and Byzantine archaeology, long-term site monitoring, and climatic risk analysis for open-air ruins.
    \item \textbf{Eötvös Loránd Tudományegyetem (ELTE BTK):} Supplies specialists in digital archaeology, digital humanities, and AI-based heritage processing from the National Laboratory for Digital Heritage.
    \item \textbf{Sorbonne Université:} Brings leading scholars from the Institut d'art et d'archéologie, focusing on medieval and early modern architectural history and data collection.
    \item \textbf{University of Bologna (Unibo) [Optional]:} Offers top-tier European expertise in medieval studies and heritage digitization via the FrameLab project.
\end{itemize}
